Descriptions of Variables
DDV(me)

Characterizes the change in molar volume between the NPs used in the new output and reference states, considering the measures of toxic effects

DDL(me)

Accounts for the variation in size between the NPs used in the new and reference states, considering the measures of toxic effects.

DDµ 1(ATO)

Characterizes the change in spectral moment of order 3 (weighted by polarizability) between the NPs used in the new and reference states, with consideration of the shapes of the NPs.

DDµ 3(POL)ns

Describes the variation in electronegativity between the NPs used in the new and reference states, depending on the conditions under which the sizes of the NPs were measured.

DDE(dm)

A perturbation term that accounts for the difference in the spectral moment of order 1, which is weighted by the atomic weight between the NPs used in the new and reference states. This variable is influenced by the biological systems involved in the analysis.

DDµ 3(VAN)ta

Accounts for the difference in the spectral moment of order 3 (weighted by atomic van der Waals radius) between the NPs used in the new and reference states, considering the exposure times

DDµ 2(ATO)ta

A perturbation term describing how the spectral moment of order two changes (weighted by atomic weight) between the new and reference states of the NPs, accounting for the exposure times involved in the experiment.

DGµ 2(HYD)sc

Represents the general spectral moment of order two weighted by hydrophobicity, which accounts for the difference between the chemical structures of the coating agents used in the new and reference states.

DGµ 5(PSA)sc

Represents the general spectral moment of order five weighted by polar surface area, which characterizes the difference between the chemical structures of the coating agents used in the new and reference states

Toxi(cj)r

A dummy classification variable that defines the toxic impact of the NP used in the reference state.
